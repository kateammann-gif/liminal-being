Liminal
Being

This project is an audio experience that came into being as result of my internal process of finding identity between two very contrasting cultures.

It aims to convey the feeling of being in between two contradicting spaces, it is an invite to hold space for tension and contradiction, comfort and discomfort. This work symbolizes the feeling of being born into the liminal space between two very different cultures and growing up to learn what it means to constantly balance the two and living with the tension of being in between. This app is an invite to follow the process of deep listening, a practice that the artist applied while researching for the sounds that were formative in the artist's upbringing. It weaves these sounds into the existing soundscape of Zurich, where the artist currently resides.

How to Listen.

This project guides you to 12 different locations in Zurich where I spent most of my time and are special to me. Each location is linked to a different audio experience. This app requires access to your phone's Geo Data.

You can then move into the vicinity of one of the locations (there is no particular order to access the audio piece). During listening, you may move within a 200 meter parameter of the chosen location. Once you leave the location, the linked audio piece will no longer be available.

For the full experience, it is recommended to put on a pair of headphones and settle down for the duration of the audio piece.

Get to know
the Project.

This project is a passion project on many layers.

I learned a couple of years ago that sound can be a medium of transportation to other places and other times. I realized that I could bring people along with travels without them ever being in this place with me. Other than pictures or film, it is much more subtle and leaves room for imagining worlds and stories and I found that I could convey experiences that were impossible for me to put into words.

On my research trip to Thailand my aim was to look for the sounds that were most formative in my childhood and youth. I wanted to collect these sounds and bring them back to Switzerland to finally show the people in my hometown in Zurich a part of me that I had kept hidden for almost all of my life.

So in some sense, this art project is a return to my roots in Switzerland and in Thailand and my attempt to take ownership of this liminal space that I am filling.
